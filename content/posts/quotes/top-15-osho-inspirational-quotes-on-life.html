Introduction

Bhagwan Shree Rajneesh, commonly known as Osho, Acharya Rajneesh and Rajneesh, was an Indian tantrik and leader of the Rajneesh movement. During his lifetime, he was considered a controversial mystic, guru, and spiritual teacher.

In the 1960s, he toured across India as a public speaker and was also an outspoken critic of Mahatma Gandhi and Hindu religious orthodoxy. He also used to express his views on human sexuality in public places, which is why he was often called "Sex Guru".

He became a lecturer in philosophy at Jabalpur University in 1958 and was later promoted to the rank of professor in 1960. Along with being his teacher, he used to give his spiritual lectures in the form of "Acharya Rajneesh" in the states of India.

He opposed socialism and felt that India could only prosper through capitalism, science, technology, and birth control. He raised a variety of issues in his speeches, such as criticizing orthodox Indian religion and rituals and said that sex is the first step towards achieving spiritual growth.
