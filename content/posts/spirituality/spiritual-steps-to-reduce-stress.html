What is Stress?

Stress is a natural response that your body has to challenges or demands. It's a part of life, and in certain situations, stress can be beneficial as it can help you respond to threats or motivate you to achieve your goals. However, when stress becomes chronic or overwhelming, it can have negative effects on your physical and mental well-being.

Common Causes of Stress

  1. Work-related issues: Pressure at the workplace, deadlines, conflicts, and high expectations can contribute to stress.
  2. Financial problems: Economic difficulties, debt, or financial instability can be significant stressors.
  3. Relationship issues: Conflicts with family, friends, or romantic partners can lead to stress.
  4. Health concerns: Serious illnesses, chronic health conditions, or the health issues of loved ones can cause stress.
  5. Life changes: Major life events such as moving, getting married, divorce, or the death of a loved one can be stressful.
  6. Uncertainty and lack of control: Feeling a lack of control or uncertainty about the future can contribute to stress.
  7. Overload and burnout: Taking on too many responsibilities without adequate self-care can lead to burnout and stress.
  8. Environmental factors: Noise, pollution, or other environmental factors can contribute to stress.
